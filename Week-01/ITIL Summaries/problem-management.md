PROBLEM MANAGEMENT
What is the practice?
Problem Management is the ITIL practice responsible for identifying and managing the root causes of incidents to prevent them from happening again or to reduce their impact.
Problem management aims at two things: First, it aims to completely eliminate recurring incidents so that the same problem does not keep disrupting services repeatedly. Second, for problems that cannot be fully prevented, it works to reduce and minimize the impact they have whenever they do occur, so that the business and its users are affected as little as possible.

Why it matters
Problem management matters because those same small, recurring incidents are often clues pointing to a deeper, underlying issue. If problem management doesn't pay attention to minor incidents happening repeatedly, they risk missing the chance to identify the root cause early, which means the same small annoyance keeps happening until it eventually grows into something more serious.

What goes wrong when it's missing or done poorly?
When problem management is missing or poorly executed, recurring issues are never properly investigated or permanently resolved. Instead of digging into the root cause, teams keep applying quick temporary fixes every time the same problem surfaces, wasting time and resources without ever addressing what is actually causing it. Over time, these unresolved issues grow and worsen until they reach a point where even the temporary fix no longer works, resulting in a far more serious situation than what was originally there.

One real example from our institution – AWWDA
A practical example of this is when the office Wi-Fi keeps disconnecting repeatedly, and each time it happens, the IT team simply restarts the router to restore the connection temporarily. While this gets things working again in the short term, no one investigates why the disconnections keep occurring. Because the root cause is never addressed, the problem continues until eventually the Wi-Fi goes off completely and restarting the router no longer works. The entire office is left without internet connectivity, bringing all work to a standstill, a situation that could have been completely avoided had the root cause been properly identified and resolved from the beginning.
