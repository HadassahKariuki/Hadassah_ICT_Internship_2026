Service Request Management
1. What is this practice?
Service Request Management is the practice of handling user requests for services in a structured and efficient manner. These requests are usually standard and pre-approved, such as password resets, software installation, access to systems, or requests for new equipment. The goal is to ensure that users receive the services they need quickly and consistently.

2. Why does it matter?
Service Request Management is important because it ensures that users can easily request IT services and receive them without unnecessary delays. It improves efficiency, keeps requests organized and tracked, and helps employees get the tools and access they need to perform their jobs effectively. It also increases user satisfaction by providing a clear and reliable process for fulfilling requests.

3. What goes wrong when it's missing or done poorly?
When Service Request Management is poorly managed, requests are delayed, forgotten, or handled inconsistently. Employees may wait a long time for software, system access, or equipment needed to do their work. This leads to reduced productivity, frustration among staff, and inefficiencies within the organization.

4. One real example from our institution – AWWDA
An employee may require specific software, such as Microsoft Office, Adobe Acrobat, or a specialized application needed to perform their duties. Instead of installing the software themselves, the employee submits a request to the Service Desk. The request is recorded, reviewed, and approved where necessary. The ICT team then installs and configures the software on the employee's computer and confirms once the installation is complete. This process ensures that only authorized software is installed and that employees have the tools they need to perform their work effectively.
