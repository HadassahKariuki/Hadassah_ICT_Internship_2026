INCIDENT MANAGEMENT
What is this practice?
Incident Management is all about dealing with unexpected problems that suddenly pop up and disrupt IT services. Whenever something goes wrong, the first step is to identify what the issue is, write it down, and then work as fast as possible to fix it and get things back to normal. 
The main goal is simply to restore the service and make sure that users are not affected for too long, so they can get back to doing their work without too much interruption. 
The main aim to ensure that the services are restored and the users can go on with their immediate job even if the root cause is still not identified

Why does it matter?
It is essential to closely monitor day-to-day operations because even minor incidents can quickly escalate, leading to reduced service availability and lower customer satisfaction. 
Paying attention to small details can have a significant positive impact.

What goes wrong when it's missing or done poorly?
When incident management is missing or poorly executed, issues that arise within the organization go undetected and unresolved for far longer than they should. Because there is no proper process in place to identify and address problems promptly, small issues are given the opportunity to grow and escalate into much larger and more damaging situations. Critical information and resources that employees depend on to carry out their daily tasks become unavailable or permanently lost, leaving staff unable to perform their work efficiently. This brings productivity to a halt and causes widespread disruption across the organization, affecting not just individual employees but entire departments and business operations as a whole.

One real example from our institution – AWWDA
A clear example of this is when an employee is saving their work on OneDrive, but due to a lack of proper, nobody notices that OneDrive has stopped syncing properly. The employee continues working, assuming everything is being backed up as normal, unaware that their files are no longer being saved to the cloud. 
When the computer suddenly crashes or gets physically damaged beyond repair, all of the critical files and documents that were never successfully synced are permanently lost with no way of recovering them. This loss of crucial data not only disrupts the employee's work but also has a wider negative impact on the department and the organization as a whole, especially if the lost information was tied to ongoing projects or important business operations. 
Had there been proper communication and monitoring systems in place between departments, the syncing issue could have been detected and resolved early before it resulted in such a significant and damaging loss of data.
