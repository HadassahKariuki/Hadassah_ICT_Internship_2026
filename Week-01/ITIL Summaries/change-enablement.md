CHANGE ENABLEMENT
1. What is this practice?
Change Enablement is the process of making sure that any changes made to IT systems and infrastructure are handled in an organized, controlled, and consistent manner.
 It covers three types of changes: standard changes, which are low risk and pre-approved; normal changes, which are broader and require proper review and authorization before being carried out; and emergency changes, which need to happen immediately to resolve a critical issue or security threat.

2. Why does it matter?
Change management matters because its main goal is to keep IT services running smoothly, especially the ones the business depends on most, while reducing the negative effects of changes that fail or are poorly executed. 
Without it, changes are made in a disorganized and uncontrolled way, greatly increasing the chances of something going wrong and disrupting business operations.

3. What goes wrong when it's missing or done poorly?
When change management is absent or poorly carried out, changes get implemented without proper planning, testing, or approval, making it far more likely that something will break or malfunction. 
Organizations without a proper process tend to experience more unauthorized changes, unexpected emergency fixes, and failed changes that need to be reversed, leading to more incidents, more service disruptions, and a negative impact on the employees and customers who depend on those services every day.

One real example from our institution – AWWDA
At AWWDA, we recently went through an upgrade from Windows 10 to Windows 11 following a proper change enablement process. 
The IT team ensured all devices were compatible, ran tests on selected machines, and carried out the upgrade after hours to keep disruptions to a minimum. The stronger malware protection and tighter access controls that come built into Windows 11 are expected to make it considerably harder for unauthorized individuals to gain access to company systems and sensitive data. 
It is also anticipated that employees will experience better device performance and faster response times, allowing them to carry out their daily tasks more efficiently and productively.
